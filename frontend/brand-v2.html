Hi. We're a parking
enforcement company. (we promise it's actually fine.)

Most people hear "tow company" and tense up. Fair. The category is full of operators who'd rather you just paid the fee. This brand book is for the other case — where the car genuinely shouldn't be there, the people who live there shouldn't be inconvenienced, and the tow shouldn't show up unless it actually has to.

— a brand book, written by the people who installed the cameras.

05 / rules of thumb

Do & don't.

If a thing on the page feels like it could've come from a generic SaaS dashboard or a generic tow-company flyer, it's wrong. We're neither.

✓ DO

Talk like a real human wrote it.

Parentheticals are encouraged. Self-awareness is encouraged. The word actually is encouraged when it earns its keep.

✓ DO

Acknowledge the weirdness of the category.

Most people hate tow companies. We don't pretend we don't know that. Naming it is the only thing that builds trust.

✓ DO

Use italics like a highlighter.

Fraunces italics carry the warmth. Drop one or two per headline. Earn each one. Italicizing every other word is a different brand.

✓ DO

Lean into printed details.

Plate numbers, timestamps, lat-lon, evidence pack IDs. Set them in DM Mono. Treat them as the receipts they are.

✗ DON'T

Don't go corporate.

No "AI-powered SaaS solution." No "synergize." No "unlock value." If a phrase has appeared in a B2B deck before, find a different one.

✗ DON'T

Don't fearmonger about towing.

"Crack down on violators!" energy is the old industry. We're the alternative. Our trick is fairness, not fear.

✗ DON'T

Don't add emojis in product copy.

Stamps and line drawings carry the warmth. Emojis cheapen it. The difference is small but real.

✗ DON'T

Don't go back to charcoal + amber.

That was the v1 brand. It's a fine brand. It's not this brand. Cream paper is the load-bearing decision.
